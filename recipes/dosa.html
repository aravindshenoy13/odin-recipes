Masala Dosa Recipe

Description

This is the recipe to a mouth-watering masala dosa you can make for today's lunch!

Ingredients

  • Lentils - Urad dal, Chana dal
  • Rice
  • Poha
  • Methi Seeds
  • Cold Water
  • Potatoes - Boiled and mashed
  • Green Chillis- 1 or 2
  • Corainder Leaves
  • Onions - about 200g
  • Cashews - optional
  • Spices for taste

Steps

  1. First take all the ingredients in three bowls.
    • 1.5 cups idli rice or parboiled rice in one bowl. You can also use regular short-grained to medium-grained rice like sona masuri or parmal.
    • ½ cup urad dal (husked black gram) + 1 tablespoon chana dal (husked and split bengal gram) + 20 methi seeds (fenugreek seeds) in a second bowl
    • ⅓ cup thick poha (flattened rice) in a third bowl.
  2. Rinse the lentils and fenugreek seeds a couple of times. Then soak them in 1 cup water for 4 to 5 hours.
  3. Rinse the rice a couple of times and keep aside.
  4. Rinse flattened rice once or twice and then add to the rice.
  5. Pour 2 cups water. Stir and soak both rice and flattened rice together for 4 to 5 hours.
  6. Before grinding, strain the water from the soaked lentils+fenugreek seeds and keep it for grinding urad dal. You can also use fresh water if you prefer.
  7. Add the lentils in the grinder jar. Also add ½ cup of the soaked and strained water to the lentils or ½ cup of fresh water if you like.
  8. Grind the urad dal, chana dal and fenugreek seeds till you get a batter which is light and fluffy. The urad dal has to be ground really well, so that the dosa batter ferments well.
  9. Remove batter in a bowl or pan with a spatula. Below is a photo showing the consistency of the urad dal batter.
  10. Strain the rice well. Then in the same grinder, add the soaked rice and 1 cup fresh water. You can grind rice in one batch or in two to three batches. This will depend on the size of the grinder jar.
  11. Grind the rice till you get a fine grainy consistency in it. The rice batter should have a fine rava (cream of wheat) like consistency of the ground rice grains.
  12. Now pour the rice+flattened rice batter in the same pan or bowl containing the urad lentil batter.
  13. Add ½ teaspoon edible rock salt or add as per taste. You can also use sea salt or pink salt.
  14. Mix the salt very well with the ground batter. Also, mix both the rice batter and the urad lentil batter thoroughly. Cover with a lid and keep aside to ferment for 8 to 9 hours at room temperature.
  15. Rinse and then boil 4 medium sized potatoes in 2 cups water for 5 to 6 whistles in a pressure cooker.
  16. When the potatoes are cooking, rinse and soak 1 tablespoon chana dal in ¼ cup hot water for 30 minutes. Then drain all the water from the chana dal and set aside.
  17. When the pressure settles down on its own in the cooker, remove the lid. Drain the potatoes and let them become warm.
  18. Then peel and chop them. Also, thinly slice 2 medium to large onions (200 grams) and chop 1 to 2 green chilies, finely chop 1 inch ginger and some coriander leaves.
  19. Heat oil or ghee in a frying pan or skillet. If using cashews, then fry 2 tablespoons cashews at this step and keep them aside. Then add the chana dal
  20. Saute the chana dal for 2 to 3 minutes on a low heat or till they turn light golden or golden and add the sliced onions, curry leaves, green chilies and ginger.
  21. Sauté the onions till they soften and turn translucent. Stir often while sautéing onions so that they get evenly cooked.
  22. Add the turmeric powder and asafoetida (hing). To make it gluten-free use the gluten-free asafoetida or skip it altogether.
  23. Mix very well and add ½ cup water
  24. Again mix well and simmer for 2 to 3 minutes or till the mixture thickens a bit.
  25. Next add the boiled chopped potatoes and mix well.
  26. Season with salt according to taste. Also add ¼ to ½ teaspoon sugar for a slightly sweet taste. You can add more sugar if you prefer.
  27. Simmer on a low to medium-low heat for 3 to 4 minutes stirring occasionally.
  28. Switch off the heat and then add chopped coriander leaves. Also add fried cashews now (if you have fried the cashews earlier as mentioned in step 5 above). Mix well.
  29. Stir and keep the potato masala aside. The potato filling should be moist and easily spreadable on the dosa.
  30. Now lightly stir the batter, before you begin to make dosa. You will also see tiny air pockets in the batter with an increase in its volume.
  31. eat a cast iron pan or a flat non-stick pan. The pan should be medium hot. Smear some oil if using an iron pan or griddle. Don't smear oil on a non stick pan, as then you won't be able to spread the batter easily.
  32. Keep the heat to a low, while spreading batter. Spread the batter in a circular way on the pan. On a medium heat, cook the dosa.
  33. Cook till its base becomes golden and crisp. You can spread the oil which was sprinkled earlier on the dosa with a spoon.
  34. When you see the base has become golden, the edges have separated from the pan and the dosa has cooked, place a portion of the potato masala filling on the dosa. You can spread the filling a bit if you want.
